Startup Weekend

In January I attended Dublin Student Startup Weekend at Google. It was a great chance to meet some amazing people and see how quickly an idea can be developed with the right team. In 54 hours we developed Studio. Studio was created to solve two issues. Firstly; students may pick up skills through their college course but are not always afforded opportunities to use these professionally as they have little work experience. Secondly, businesses may want to leverage skilled professionals in areas such as web design, application development or photography, but cannot afford them. Studio is a platform to link students with businesses to solve these issues. The value that Studio provides to the business is the vetting of students to ensure that they are skilled enough, and the value for students is the management of the relationship between them and the business.

HoloLens Project

In 2019 as part of a college software engineering project, I got to partner with Microsoft and develop an app for the HoloLens. When my team found out that we were chosen for the mixed reality project, we decided it would be a great opportunity to make a navigation app. There were no apps like this on the HoloLens and we saw plenty of opportunity to explore areas like speech-to-text, text-to-speech and drawing of augmented reality objects as well as fixed objects in the field of vision. We were delighted with the end result. By the end of the ten weeks our app could take voice input of the destination, repeat it as voice output, decode the coordinates, decide a route and guide the user there in augmented reality! The app passed all test cases in the emulator but was a bit slow when testing on the real thing due to the hardware limitations of the first HoloLens. Nevertheless we got a particular test case working on the actual hardware, which you can see in the video!
